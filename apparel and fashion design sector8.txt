Apparel & Fashion Design

1. Fashion Designing (Diploma)
Objective: Develop creative and technical skills to design clothing collections, understand market trends, and prepare for professional fashion industry roles. Duration: 12-18 Months (Diploma Program) Prerequisites: 10+2 education; basic drawing skills preferred; passion for fashion Career Pathways: Fashion Designer, Collection Designer, Design Director, Fashion Consultant, Freelance Designer
Module 1: Fashion History & Trend Analysis (Weeks 1-6)
•	History of fashion: Ancient to contemporary periods
•	Fashion movements and eras
•	Influential designers and their contributions
•	Trend forecasting tools and resources (WGSN, Trendstop, Fashion Forecasting agencies)
•	Seasonal trend analysis (Spring/Summer, Fall/Winter)
•	Practical: Trend board creation (mood boards, color palettes, silhouette analysis)
•	Create trend forecast presentation for upcoming season
Module 2: Design Principles & Fundamentals (Weeks 7-12)
•	Elements of design: Line, shape, form, color, texture, proportion
•	Design principles: Balance, emphasis, movement, rhythm, unity
•	Visual communication through design
•	Color theory: Psychology, harmony, combinations
•	Fabric and material exploration
•	Practical: Design exercises using principles
•	Create 20 design sketches exploring different silhouettes
•	Color palette development for collections
•	Fabric sampling and mood boards
Module 3: Fashion Illustration & Figure Drawing (Weeks 13-20)
•	Fashion figure proportions (9-head fashion figure)
•	Different fashion figure poses: Standing, sitting, walking, action poses
•	Quick sketching techniques
•	Fabric rendering: Cotton, silk, denim, leather, chiffon
•	Garment illustration: Tops, bottoms, dresses, outerwear
•	Mixed media techniques (pencil, markers, watercolor, digital)
•	Practical: Draw 50 fashion figures in various poses
•	Illustrate 30 garments with fabric details
•	Create collection presentation boards (8-10 designs)
Module 4: Garment Design & Construction (Weeks 21-28)
•	Basic garment blocks (bodice, sleeve, skirt, pant)
•	Design variations and modifications
•	Seam finishes and construction details
•	Pattern grading concepts
•	Practical: Construct 5 basic garments (shirt, skirt, pant, dress, jacket)
•	Create design specifications sheets
•	Develop pattern variations for different sizes
Module 5: Collection Development (Weeks 29-36)
•	Collection themes and concepts
•	Market research and target audience analysis
•	Designing cohesive collections (8-12 pieces)
•	Color and texture coordination
•	Commercial viability assessment
•	Practical: Design complete collection (theme + 10-12 designs)
•	Create collection presentation (boards, mood boards, swatches)
•	Develop design specifications for production
Module 6: CAD & Digital Design (Weeks 37-42)
•	Introduction to fashion design software: Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop
•	Digital sketching tools: Procreate, ClipStudio Paint
•	CAD software basics: Browzwear, Clo3D, Modaris
•	2D flat sketches and technical drawings
•	3D garment visualization
•	Practical: Digitize hand sketches
•	Create 10 designs in CAD software
•	3D visualization of collection pieces
Module 7: Production & Sourcing (Weeks 43-48)
•	Garment production process
•	Cost estimation and pricing
•	Sourcing materials and suppliers
•	Quality control basics
•	Sustainability in fashion
•	Practical: Develop costing sheet for garments
•	Create production timeline
•	Supplier research and evaluation
Module 8: Portfolio Development (Weeks 49-54)
•	Building professional portfolio
•	Digital and physical presentation
•	Creating lookbook
•	Social media presence for designers
•	Interview preparation
•	Practical: Complete professional portfolio
•	Create digital portfolio/website
•	Develop personal brand identity
Assessment:
•	Continuous design projects (40%)
•	Fashion illustration portfolio (25%)
•	Collection development project (25%)
•	Final presentations and viva (10%)
Internship: 2-3 months with fashion designer/apparel brand
Certification: Diploma in Fashion Design
