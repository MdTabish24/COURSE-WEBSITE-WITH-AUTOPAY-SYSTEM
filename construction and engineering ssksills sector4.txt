Construction & Engineering Skills

1. Electrician (Industrial & Domestic)
•	Objective: To train students in wiring, installation, and maintenance of electrical systems in homes and factories.
•	Duration: 6 Months to 1 Year
Module 1: Safety & Tools
•	Occupational Health & Safety (PPE, Fire Extinguishers types).
•	First Aid for electric shock.
•	Identification and use of hand tools (Pliers, Screwdrivers, Wire strippers, Test lamp).
•	Power tools (Drilling machine, Grinders).
Module 2: Basic Electricity
•	Voltage, Current, Resistance, Power (Ohm’s Law).
•	AC vs. DC current.
•	Conductors, Insulators, and Semiconductors.
•	Series and Parallel circuits.
Module 3: Domestic Wiring (House Wiring)
•	Types of wires and cables (Casing-capping, Conduit wiring).
•	Switchboard connection: Switches, Sockets, Fan regulators, Fuses, MCBs.
•	Earthing: Pipe earthing, Plate earthing, importance of Grounding.
•	Home Appliances repair: Iron, Geyser, Heater, Ceiling Fan (Winding basics).
•	Inverter and Battery connection.
Module 4: Industrial Wiring & Motor Control
•	3-Phase Supply vs. Single Phase.
•	Power wiring vs. Control wiring.
•	Motors: AC Induction motors, DC motors.
•	Starters: DOL (Direct Online), Star-Delta Starter, Soft Starters.
•	Panel Board wiring and reading electrical diagrams.
•	Transformers: Basics, maintenance, and oil testing.
Module 5: Measuring Instruments
•	Using a Multimeter (Digital/Analog).
•	Clamp meter (Tong tester) usage.
•	Megger (Insulation resistance tester).
•	Energy meter (Sub-meter) installation.

2. Plumbing & Sanitary Works
•	Objective: To master pipe fitting, water supply systems, and drainage for buildings.
•	Duration: 3 to 6 Months
Module 1: Introduction & Safety
•	Plumbing tools (Pipe wrench, Threading die, Pipe cutter, Hacksaw).
•	Safety precautions on construction sites.
Module 2: Pipes & Fittings
•	Types of Pipes: GI (Galvanized Iron), PVC, CPVC, UPVC, PPR, HDPE.
•	Types of Fittings: Elbow, Tee, Union, Socket, Reducer, End cap.
•	Joining methods: Threading, Solvent cementing, Fusion welding (for PPR).
Module 3: Water Supply System
•	Overhead tank installation and connection.
•	Installation of Pumps (Monoblock, Submersible).
•	Domestic water line layout (Kitchen, Bathroom).
•	Pressure testing of pipelines.
Module 4: Sanitary & Drainage System
•	Installation of Sanitary ware: Washbasins, Commodes (Western/Indian), Urinals, Sinks.
•	Traps: P-Trap, S-Trap, Bottle Trap, Gully Trap, Nahani Trap.
•	Septic Tank basics and soak pit connection.
•	Rainwater harvesting basics.
Module 5: Maintenance & Repair
•	Fixing leaking taps and valves (Gate valve, Ball valve).
•	Clearing clogged drains and pipes.
•	Washer replacement and minor repairs.

3. Welder (Arc & Gas)
•	Objective: To teach joining of metals using Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW) and Oxy-Acetylene gas.
•	Duration: 6 Months
Module 1: Workshop Safety
•	Personal Protective Equipment (Leather apron, Welding shield/goggles, Gloves).
•	Hazards: UV radiation, Fumes, Electric shock, Fire.
Module 2: Metal Preparation
•	Measuring and marking tools (Steel rule, Scriber, Try square).
•	Cutting tools (Hacksaw, Chisel).
•	Edge preparation for welding (V-groove, U-groove).
Module 3: Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW)
•	Arc welding machine setup (AC/DC polarity).
•	Electrodes: Types, coding, drying, and selection.
•	Striking an arc and bead practice.
•	Welding Positions: Flat (1G), Horizontal (2G), Vertical (3G), Overhead (4G).
•	Joints: Butt, Lap, Tee, Corner joint.
Module 4: Gas Welding & Cutting (Oxy-Acetylene)
•	Cylinders (Oxygen - Black, Acetylene - Maroon) and Regulators.
•	Flame types: Neutral, Carburizing, Oxidizing.
•	Gas welding techniques (Leftward/Rightward).
•	Gas Cutting (Pug cutting machine & Manual).
•	Brazing and Soldering basics.
Module 5: Inspection & Defects
•	Common defects: Porosity, Undercut, Slag inclusion, Cracks.
•	Visual inspection and basic Non-Destructive Testing (Dye Penetrant Test).

4. CNC Operator
•	Objective: To operate Computer Numerical Control (CNC) Lathe and Milling machines.
•	Duration: 3 to 6 Months
Module 1: Metrology (Measurement)
•	Precision instruments: Vernier Caliper, Micrometer, Height Gauge, Bore Gauge.
•	Reading technical drawings and tolerances (GD&T basics).
Module 2: Introduction to CNC
•	Difference between Manual Lathe and CNC.
•	Coordinate Systems: Absolute (G90) vs. Incremental (G91).
•	Axes: X, Y, Z directions.
Module 3: Programming (G-Codes & M-Codes)
•	G-Codes: G00 (Rapid), G01 (Linear), G02/G03 (Circular), G71 (Cycles).
•	M-Codes: M03 (Spindle ON), M05 (Spindle OFF), M08 (Coolant ON), M30 (End).
•	Writing simple programs for Facing, Turning, Tapering.
Module 4: Machine Operation (Practical)
•	Control Panel operation (Fanuc/Siemens).
•	Work Offsetting: Taking Work Zero (G54).
•	Tool Offsetting: Measuring tool geometry.
•	Loading/Unloading workpieces.
•	Dry run and Single block execution.
Module 5: Maintenance
•	Coolant management.
•	Lubrication checks.
•	Chip removal and daily housekeeping.

5. HVAC (AC & Refrigerator) Technician
•	Objective: Installation, service, and repair of Air Conditioners and Refrigerators.
•	Duration: 6 Months
Module 1: Thermodynamics & Heat
•	Modes of heat transfer (Conduction, Convection, Radiation).
•	Temperature scales and pressure units.
•	Refrigeration Cycle: Compressor, Condenser, Expansion Valve, Evaporator.
Module 2: Components & Tools
•	Tools: Tube cutter, Flaring tool, Swaging tool, Manifold gauge, Vacuum pump.
•	Refrigerants: R-134a, R-22, R-32, R-410A, R-600a (Safety & Flammability).
•	Brazing copper tubes.
Module 3: Domestic Refrigerators
•	Single door (Direct Cool) vs. Double door (Frost Free).
•	Electrical wiring: Relay, OLP (Overload Protector), Thermostat, Timer, Heater.
•	Gas charging procedure (Vacuuming, Charging, Sealing).
Module 4: Air Conditioning (Window & Split)
•	Installation: Indoor unit mounting, Outdoor unit placement, Piping, Wiring.
•	Inverter AC Technology: PCB basics, Error codes.
•	Pump down method (Gas locking).
•	Servicing: Filter cleaning, Jet pump cleaning (Wet service).
Module 5: Troubleshooting
•	Leak detection (Nitrogen pressure test, Soap solution).
•	Common faults: Not cooling, Water leakage, High Ampere, Noise.

6. Draftsman (AutoCAD)
•	Objective: To create 2D and 3D technical drawings for Civil and Mechanical engineering.
•	Duration: 3 Months
Module 1: Introduction to AutoCAD
•	User Interface, Workspace, Command line.
•	Coordinate systems.
•	File formats (.dwg, .dxf).
Module 2: Basic Drawing & Editing
•	Draw Commands: Line, Polyline, Circle, Arc, Rectangle, Polygon, Hatch.
•	Modify Commands: Move, Copy, Rotate, Mirror, Trim, Extend, Fillet, Offset, Array.
Module 3: Layers, Dimensions & Text
•	Layer Management: Creating layers, colors, lineweights.
•	Dimensioning: Linear, Aligned, Radius, Diameter, Angular.
•	Text: Single line and Multi-line text.
•	Blocks: Creating and inserting blocks (Doors, Windows, Furniture).
Module 4: Civil/Mechanical Projects
•	Civil: Floor plans, Elevations, Sections of a building.
•	Mechanical: Machine parts, Orthographic projections.
Module 5: Plotting & 3D Basics
•	Page setup and Printing (Plotting to PDF).
•	Introduction to 3D: Extrude, Revolve, Union, Subtract.

7. Civil Construction Supervisor
•	Objective: To supervise construction sites, manage labor, and ensure quality.
•	Duration: 6 Months to 1 Year
Module 1: Construction Materials
•	Cement (Grades 33, 43, 53), Sand (River vs. M-Sand), Aggregates.
•	Bricks (Red bricks, Fly ash, AAC blocks).
•	Steel reinforcement (TMT bars).
Module 2: Reading Drawings
•	Reading Architectural and Structural drawings.
•	Understanding Scale and Gridlines.
•	Centerline marking and Layout on ground.
Module 3: Site Execution Basics
•	Excavation: Soil types, Shoring.
•	PCC & RCC: Concrete Mix Design (M15, M20, M25), Water-Cement ratio.
•	Formwork (Shuttering): Checking levels and plumb (verticality).
•	Bar Bending Schedule (BBS): Cutting length, Stirrups, Lapping zones.
•	Masonry & Plastering: Brick bonds, Curing methods.
Module 4: Surveying Basics
•	Using Auto Level and Dump_y Level for transferring levels.
•	Measuring tape techniques.
Module 5: Site Management
•	Labor handling and Muster roll.
•	Daily Progress Report (DPR).
•	Quality Control: Slump test, Cube test.
•	Safety supervision (Scaffolding safety).

8. Solar Panel Installation Technician
•	Objective: To install and maintain Photovoltaic (PV) Solar Systems.
•	Duration: 3 to 4 Months
Module 1: Solar Energy Basics
•	Photovoltaic effect.
•	Solar Irradiance and Shadow analysis.
•	Types of Systems: On-Grid, Off-Grid, Hybrid.
Module 2: System Components
•	Solar Panels: Monocrystalline vs. Polycrystalline.
•	Inverters: String inverters, Micro-inverters.
•	Batteries: Lead-acid, Tubular, Lithium-ion.
•	Charge Controllers (PWM vs. MPPT).
•	Mounting Structures.
Module 3: Site Survey & Design
•	Roof assessment (Strength and Direction - South facing).
•	Load calculation (How many panels needed?).
•	Shadow free area calculation.
Module 4: Installation Procedure (Practical)
•	Fixing mounting structure (Legs/Rails).
•	Panel mounting and clamping.
•	Wiring: DC cabling, MC4 connectors, Crimping.
•	Earthing & Protection: Lightning Arrester (LA), DCDB, ACDB boxes.
Module 5: Commissioning & Maintenance
•	System testing (Voltage/Current check).
•	Inverter configuration.
•	Cleaning of panels.
•	Troubleshooting low power generation.
