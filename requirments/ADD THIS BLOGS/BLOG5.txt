🔬 What is a Medical Lab Technician?
A Medical Lab Technician (MLT) is a healthcare professional responsible for performing laboratory tests that help doctors diagnose, treat, and prevent diseases. They work with various biological specimens including blood, urine, tissue samples, and other body fluids to provide accurate diagnostic information.
Key Responsibilities of Medical Lab Technicians
●	Sample Collection: Safely collecting blood, urine, and other biological specimens from patients
●	Laboratory Testing: Conducting various diagnostic tests using sophisticated equipment and instruments
●	Data Analysis: Analyzing test results and ensuring accuracy of findings
●	Quality Control: Maintaining laboratory standards and ensuring proper calibration of equipment
●	Record Keeping: Maintaining detailed records of test results and patient information
●	Equipment Maintenance: Regular cleaning and maintenance of laboratory instruments
●	Safety Protocols: Following strict safety guidelines and infection control procedures

Course Types and Duration

Duration: 2 years full-time program
Eligibility: 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology (PCB) with minimum 50% marks
Focus: Comprehensive training in all aspects of medical laboratory technology
Certificate Courses
Duration: 6 months to 1 year
Eligibility: 10+2 with science subjects
Focus: Basic laboratory skills and specific diagnostic techniques
Bachelor of Science in Medical Lab Technology (B.Sc. MLT)
Duration: 3 years undergraduate program
Eligibility: 10+2 with PCB and minimum 50% marks
Focus: Advanced theoretical knowledge and research-oriented training
Core Curriculum Components
1.	Clinical Biochemistry: Blood chemistry, enzyme analysis, and metabolic testing
2.	Hematology: Blood cell analysis, coagulation studies, and blood disorders
3.	Microbiology: Bacterial, viral, and fungal identification and testing
4.	Pathology: Tissue examination and cellular analysis
5.	Immunology: Immune system testing and antibody detection
6.	Clinical Chemistry: Chemical analysis of body fluids and specimens
7.	Laboratory Management: Quality control, safety protocols, and administrative procedures
8.	Practical Training: Hands-on experience with modern laboratory equipment
