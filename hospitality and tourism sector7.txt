Hospitality & Tourism


1. Hotel Management
Objective: Develop comprehensive knowledge of hotel operations, management principles, and guest service excellence across all departments. Duration: 12 Months (Diploma Program) Prerequisites: 10+2 education; basic English communication skills Career Pathways: Hotel Manager, Operations Manager, Department Head, Hospitality Consultant
Module 1: Introduction to Hospitality Industry (Weeks 1-4)
•	History and evolution of hospitality industry
•	Types of accommodation: Hotels, Resorts, Motels, Boutique Hotels
•	Hotel classification systems (Star rating, AAA Diamond)
•	Organizational structure of hotels
•	Practical: Visit to 3 different hotel categories, organizational chart creation
Module 2: Front Office Operations (Weeks 5-10)
•	Reservation systems: Manual vs. Computerized (PMS - Property Management Systems)
•	Guest cycle: Pre-arrival, Arrival, Occupancy, Departure
•	Room tariff structures, yield management
•	Night audit procedures
•	Practical: Opera PMS training, mock check-in/check-out procedures
Module 3: Housekeeping Management (Weeks 11-14)
•	Cleaning procedures for different areas
•	Linen and laundry management
•	Inventory control systems
•	Pest control and waste management
•	Practical: Bed-making demonstrations, cleaning schedule preparation
Module 4: Food & Beverage Management (Weeks 15-20)
•	Restaurant types and concepts
•	Menu planning and engineering
•	Cost control and food costing
•	Beverage management and bar operations
•	Practical: Menu costing exercise, restaurant layout design
Module 5: Sales & Marketing (Weeks 21-24)
•	Hospitality marketing mix
•	Revenue management strategies
•	Digital marketing for hotels
•	Banquet sales and event management
•	Practical: Create marketing plan for a new hotel, rate card development
Module 6: Human Resources & Training (Weeks 25-28)
•	Recruitment and selection in hospitality
•	Training methodologies
•	Performance appraisal systems
•	Labor laws and regulations
•	Practical: Conduct mock interviews, design training module
Module 7: Financial Management (Weeks 29-32)
•	Hospitality accounting principles
•	Budget preparation and control
•	Ratio analysis for hotels
•	Capital investment decisions
•	Practical: Hotel P&L statement analysis, budget preparation
Module 8: Strategic Management (Weeks 33-36)
•	SWOT analysis for hotels
•	Quality management systems
•	Crisis management
•	Sustainability in hospitality
•	Practical: Develop business plan for hotel expansion
Assessment: Theory exams (40%), Practical demonstrations (30%), Project work (20%), Internship evaluation (10%) Internship: 3-month mandatory internship in 4-star or above hotel Certification: Diploma in Hotel Management

2. Culinary Arts (Professional Chef)
Objective: Master professional cooking techniques, kitchen management, and menu development for various cuisines. Duration: 9 Months (Certificate Program) Prerequisites: Basic understanding of cooking; passion for food Career Pathways: Chef de Partie, Sous Chef, Executive Chef, Food Consultant
Module 1: Culinary Fundamentals (Weeks 1-4)
•	Kitchen organization and brigade system
•	Knife skills and cuts (brunoise, julienne, batonnet)
•	Basic cooking methods (moist heat, dry heat, combination)
•	Food safety and sanitation (HACCP)
•	Practical: Knife skills assessment, basic stocks preparation
Module 2: Stocks, Sauces & Soups (Weeks 5-8)
•	Mother sauces: Béchamel, Velouté, Espagnole, Tomato, Hollandaise
•	Derivative sauces
•	Clear and thick soups
•	Consommé preparation
•	Practical: Prepare 5 mother sauces, 10 soup varieties
Module 3: Garde Manger (Weeks 9-12)
•	Cold kitchen operations
•	Salad preparations and dressings
•	Charcuterie: Pâtés, terrines, galantines
•	Canapés and hors d'oeuvres
•	Practical: Create cold buffet display, pâté preparation
Module 4: Meat & Poultry Cookery (Weeks 13-16)
•	Meat cuts and identification
•	Butchery techniques
•	Cooking methods for different meats
•	Marinades and tenderizing techniques
•	Practical: Fabricate chicken, lamb, beef cuts; prepare 15 meat dishes
Module 5: Seafood & Shellfish (Weeks 17-20)
•	Fish identification and selection
•	Fish fabrication techniques
•	Shellfish preparation
•	Cooking methods for seafood
•	Practical: Fillet different fish, prepare 10 seafood dishes
Module 6: International Cuisines (Weeks 21-28)
•	French classical cuisine
•	Italian cuisine
•	Asian cuisines (Chinese, Thai, Indian)
•	Mediterranean cuisine
•	Practical: Prepare 5 dishes from each cuisine
Module 7: Pastry & Baking Basics (Weeks 29-32)
•	Basic dough preparation
•	Cake making techniques
•	Dessert sauces and garnishes
•	Plating techniques
•	Practical: Bake 10 different desserts, plating exercises
Module 8: Kitchen Management (Weeks 33-36)
•	Menu planning and costing
•	Kitchen equipment maintenance
•	Staff scheduling
•	Inventory control
•	Practical: Create complete menu with costing, inventory sheet preparation
Assessment: Practical cooking tests (60%), Theory exams (30%), Menu planning project (10%) Industrial Training: 2 months in hotel kitchen Certification: Professional Chef Certificate
