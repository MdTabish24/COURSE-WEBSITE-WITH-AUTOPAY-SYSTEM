1. Supply Chain Management
Objective: Develop strategic understanding of end-to-end supply chain operations, optimization, and management for organizational excellence. Duration: 12 Months (Diploma Program) Prerequisites: 12th pass; basic business knowledge; computer skills Career Pathways: Supply Chain Manager, Logistics Manager, Operations Manager, Procurement Manager, Supply Chain Analyst
Module 1: Supply Chain Fundamentals (Weeks 1-4)
•	Supply chain definition and evolution
•	Supply chain components: Procurement, production, distribution, returns
•	Supply chain vs. logistics: Differences and integration
•	Supply chain networks and global trade
•	Industry benchmarks and best practices
•	Practical:
o	Map supply chains of 3 major companies
o	Case study analysis: Amazon, Apple, Unilever
o	Design simple supply chain model
o	Create supply chain flowchart
Module 2: Demand Planning & Forecasting (Weeks 5-10)
•	Demand forecasting methods: Qualitative, quantitative
•	Forecasting techniques: Time series, regression, moving average
•	Seasonal and trend analysis
•	Bullwhip effect and demand volatility
•	Advanced forecasting: Machine learning, AI applications
•	Practical:
o	Excel-based forecasting models
o	Forecast accuracy measurement
o	Demand planning software training (SAP, Kinaxis)
o	Create 12-month demand forecast
o	Scenario analysis and sensitivity testing
Module 3: Procurement & Sourcing (Weeks 11-18)
•	Procurement process: RFQ, RFP, vendor selection
•	Supplier evaluation and management
•	Purchasing strategies: Just-in-Time, VMI, Consignment
•	Negotiation techniques
•	Cost management and total cost of ownership (TCO)
•	Strategic sourcing
•	Practical:
o	Vendor scorecard creation
o	Supplier selection exercise
o	RFQ and RFP preparation
o	Negotiation simulations
o	Cost-benefit analysis
o	Supplier contract drafting
Module 4: Inventory Management (Weeks 19-26)
•	Inventory types: Raw materials, WIP, finished goods
•	Inventory models: EOQ (Economic Order Quantity), ABC analysis
•	Safety stock and service levels
•	Inventory valuation: FIFO, LIFO, weighted average
•	Inventory optimization
•	Practical:
o	ABC analysis exercise
o	EOQ calculations
o	Safety stock determination
o	Inventory turnover analysis

Module 5: Production Planning & Scheduling (Weeks 27-34)
•	Master Production Schedule (MPS)
•	Material Requirements Planning (MRP)
•	Capacity planning
•	Production scheduling techniques: FIFO, EDD, SPT
•	Lean manufacturing principles
•	Practical:
o	MRP system design
o	Production scheduling exercises
o	Capacity constraint analysis
o	Create production schedules
o	Lean process improvement projects
Module 6: Distribution & Logistics (Weeks 35-42)
•	Distribution channels: Direct, indirect, omnichannel
•	Transportation modes: Air, sea, land, rail
•	Route optimization and vehicle scheduling
•	Last-mile delivery
•	Warehouse location and network design
•	Practical:
o	Distribution network optimization

Module 7: Technology & Digital Supply Chain (Weeks 43-50)
•	ERP systems: SAP, Oracle, NetSuite
•	WMS (Warehouse Management System)
•	TMS (Transportation Management System)
•	Blockchain in supply chain
•	IoT and real-time tracking
•	Cloud-based supply chain solutions
•	Practical:
o	ERP module training (procurement, inventory, sales)
o	System data analysis

o	Create supply chain dashboard
o	Technology evaluation projects
Module 8: Risk Management & Resilience (Weeks 51-54)
