Beauty & Wellness


 1) Beautician / Skin Care Specialist
* Duration: 2 Months (Basic) | 4 Months (Advanced; includes Basic + Advanced modules)
* Delivery: 60% practical labs, 30% theory, 10% assessments
* Batch Format: 5–6 days/week, 2–3 hours/day
* Certification: Basic Certificate (2 months) or Advanced Diploma (4 months)

 A. Basic (2 Months)
 Learning Objectives
* Master salon hygiene, client consultation, threading, waxing, basic facials, manicure/pedicure, and aftercare.
* Identify skin types and recommend appropriate treatments and products.

 Module 1: Professionalism, Hygiene & Client Care (10–12 hrs)
* Topics:
  - Code of conduct, grooming standards, ethics, confidentiality.
  - Salon hygiene: cleaning, disinfection, sterilization (barbicide, UV sterilizer, autoclave basics).
  - Client intake forms, consent, patch tests, contraindications.
* Practicals:
  - Setting up a sanitized workstation.
  - Conducting consultations and patch tests (bleach/wax).
* Assessment: Hygiene SOP checklist; role-play evaluation.

 Module 2: Skin Science & Analysis (12–15 hrs)
* Topics:
  - Skin anatomy (epidermis, dermis, subcutaneous), Fitzpatrick scale, skin types/conditions.
  - Common disorders: acne, rosacea, dermatitis; when to refer to dermatologists.
* Practicals:
  - Skin analysis with magnifying lamp/woods lamp (if available).
* Assessment: Short quiz + analysis demo.

 Module 3: Hair Removal – Threading & Waxing (20–24 hrs)
* Topics:
  - Threading (brow mapping, symmetry), upper lip, chin, forehead.
  - Waxing types: strip, hot, cold, roll-on; sugaring basics; pre/post-wax care; infection control.
* Practicals:
  - Full-arm, half-leg, underarm, bikini-line demo (as per institute policy).
  - Eyebrow shaping for different face shapes.
* Assessment: Live model performance rubric (speed, accuracy, hygiene, client comfort).

 Module 4: Basic Facial & Clean-Up (24–28 hrs)
* Topics:
  - CTM protocol, steaming and safe extraction, facial massage basics (effleurage, petrissage, tapotement), masks.
  - Bleach and D-Tan: formulation strengths, exposure time, neutralization.
* Practicals:
  - Clean-up and basic facial for normal/oily/dry/sensitive skin.
* Assessment: Practical exam with case notes + aftercare guidance.

 Module 5: Hand & Foot Care (Manicure/Pedicure) (16–18 hrs)
* Topics:
  - Nail anatomy, sanitation, nail disorders (onychomycosis basics—no service policy).
  - Manicure/pedicure steps, scrubs, packs, paraffin dip (intro).
* Practicals:
  - Basic and spa manicure/pedicure, polish application, basic nail art (optional).
* Assessment: Practical checklist + client satisfaction score.

 Tools & Products (Basic)
* Tools: Towels, trolleys, magnifying lamp, steamers, extractors, threading cotton, wax heaters, manicure/pedicure kits, sanitizers/sterilizers.
* Products: Cleansers, toners, moisturizers, masks (clay/gel/cream), bleach, D-Tan, scrubs, soft wax/hard wax, cuticle softener, paraffin (optional), polish/remover.

 B. Advanced (4 Months)
Includes all Basic modules plus the following:

 Module 6: Advanced Skin Analysis & Treatment Planning (20–24 hrs)
* Topics: Detailed consultation, soap-free regimens, barrier repair, pigmentation & aging markers, sensitivity scales.
* Practicals: Customizing multi-session protocols; documenting progress with photos.
* Assessment: Written plan + viva on case selection and contraindications.

 Module 7: Advanced Facials & Peels (40–48 hrs)
* Topics:
  - Anti-aging facials (collagen-boosting, peptide/retinol basics), acne facials (non-cystic), sensitive-skin facials.
  - Premium facials (Gold, Diamond, Fruit/Herbal) and ingredient literacy.
  - Chemical peel introduction: AHA/BHA (low-strength), pH/safety, post-care; STRICT safety and referral boundaries.
* Practicals: Multi-step advanced facials; enzyme peels (non-medical grade); LED basics (intro, if available).
* Assessment: Two advanced facial cases + OSCE-style safety exam.

 Module 8: Machine-Based Skin Therapies (Intro) (28–32 hrs)
* Topics: Galvanic (disincrustation/iontophoresis), high frequency, ultrasonic skin scrubber, vacuum suction, microcurrent basics.
* Practicals: Protocol selection and safe operation per skin type.
* Assessment: Practical proficiency and device safety checklist.

 Module 9: Body Care & Spa Essentials (24–28 hrs)
* Topics: Body polishing, wraps (mud/seaweed/herbal), full-body massage basics (relaxation only), lymph drainage (intro), stretch-mark/cellulite care (cosmetic).
* Practicals: Spa service flow, room ambiance, draping, client comfort.
* Assessment: End-to-end spa service assessment.

 Module 10: Salon Operations & Retail (24–30 hrs)
* Topics: Service menu design, costings, inventory control, appointment systems, upselling ethically, complaint handling, memberships.
* Practicals: Mock front-desk operations, cross-selling role-plays.
* Assessment: Business simulation project + oral presentation.

 Portfolio & Assessment (Advanced)
* Logbook: 30+ documented services.
* Final Theory (MCQ + Short answers); Final Practical (2 complex cases).
* Certification: Advanced Beautician/Skin Care Specialist.


 2) Hair Dresser & Stylist (3 Months)
* Delivery: 70% practical, 20% theory, 10% assessments
* Target Outcomes: Precision cutting, styling, basic coloring, client care

 Module 1: Trichology & Consultation (16–20 hrs)
* Hair structure, growth cycle, porosity/elasticity tests, scalp conditions (dandruff, seborrhea).
* Client lifestyle analysis, face shapes, suitability, price/time planning.
* Tools: Scissors, razors, thinning shears, combs, clips, sanitization.

 Module 2: Shampoo, Conditioning & Scalp Care (16–18 hrs)
* Backwash ergonomics, massage techniques, deep-conditioning masks, hair spa protocols.
* Practicals: Correct product selection by hair type; hot towel therapy.

 Module 3: Foundation Haircutting (Female + Basic Male) (40–48 hrs)
* Sectioning, elevation/over-direction, guideline creation.
* Core cuts: One-length, U/V shapes, uniform layers, long layers, step-cut, bob variants (classic, graduated), fringe types.
* Basic men’s cuts: Crew, taper (intro), scissor-over-comb basics.
* Practicals: Repetition with timing targets; symmetry and finishing.

 Module 4: Styling & Thermal Techniques (28–32 hrs)
* Blow-dry finishes, round-brush techniques, straightening (temporary), curling/tonging, waves, roller sets.
* Updos: Sleek buns, messy buns, French roll, braids (classic, fishtail, Dutch).
* Heat protection, section control, finishing products.

 Module 5: Color Theory & Applications (28–32 hrs)
* Color wheel, levels/tones, developers, grey coverage rules.
* Patch test, strand test, root touch-up, global color, highlights/low-lights, balayage (intro).
* Henna application: pros/cons with chemical colors.
* Safety: PPE, scalp sensitivity, metallic dyes caution.

 Module 6: Salon Operations & Client Experience (12–16 hrs)
* Service scripts, rebooking strategies, consultation forms, pricing transparency, aftercare retailing.
* Hygiene audits and tool maintenance schedules.

 Assessments & Portfolio
* Checkpoints after Modules 3, 4, 5 (practical rubrics).
* Final: One precision cut + one styled finish + color application on live model.
* Logbook: 25+ services documented with before/after photos.


 3) Makeup Artist (Bridal & Fashion) – 2 Months
* Delivery: 65% practical, 25% theory, 10% portfolio/business
* Outcomes: Professional base, eye artistry, bridal looks, fashion/editorial basics, kit management

 Module 1: Foundations of Makeup Artistry (14–16 hrs)
* Hygiene/Sanitation: disposables, brush care, cross-contamination prevention.
* Color theory (undertones, neutral/olive/warm/cool), face shapes, lighting basics.
* Skin prep protocols for varied skin conditions.

 Module 2: Base Mastery (20–24 hrs)
* Foundation matching (oxidation, undertone), mixing, finish control (matte/dewy).
* Concealing/correcting (peach/orange/green/lavender).
* Contour/highlight (cream/powder), blush placement by face shape.
* Long-wear and transfer-resistant techniques.

 Module 3: Eye Artistry (24–28 hrs)
* Brow mapping and creation (pencil/powder/gel).
* Liners: classic, winged, smoked; waterline strategies.
* Eyeshadow: matte/satin/metallics, blending, transitions; cut-crease; halo; negative space.
* Lashes: strip/individuals; curl/mascara sequencing; glue safety.
* Cultural/photography considerations for bridal eye looks.

 Module 4: Signature Looks (Bridal & Fashion) (22–26 hrs)
* Day/corporate natural look.
* Party/evening glam; glitter application and fallout control.
* Bridal: traditional/classic, reception, contemporary HD bridal; regional draping aesthetics guidance.
* Fashion/editorial: runway-ready skin, graphic liners, trend adaptation; working with photographers.

 Module 5: Hair & Draping for MUAs (12–16 hrs)
* Quick updos, buns, waves/curls for brides.
* Veil/dupatta placement; saree drape basics for shoots.
* Accessory placement and secure pinning.

 Module 6: Professional Practice & Portfolio (12–16 hrs)
* Kit curation (sanitary, efficient, travel-ready).
* Rate cards, contracts, booking policies, trials, cancellations.
* Portfolio building: lighting, angles, retouching ethics, social media strategy.
* Client management, feedback, and referrals.

 Assessments
* Three look exams: Day look, Bridal look, Editorial/fashion look.
* Portfolio submission: 10–12 looks with product breakdowns.


 4) Cosmetology (Advanced) – 6 Months
* Integrated Program: Skin + Hair + Makeup + Nails/Body Spa + Salon Management
* Clinic Hours: Minimum 80–100 live services under supervision

 Month 1–2: Advanced Skin
* All Beautician Advanced modules plus:
  - Specialized treatments: acne (non-cystic), pigmentation management routines, non-medical peel series design, barrier-repair protocols.
  - Device integration planning (galvanic/high-frequency/ultrasonic/vacuum; LED basics).
  - Body spa modules: aromatherapy, hydrotherapy concepts (demo), wraps.
* Practicals: 15+ skin services logged (mix of facials/peels/machine add-ons).

 Month 3–4: Hair Design & Chemical Work
* Cutting mastery: precision bobs, shag/wolf cut foundations, face-framing, texturizing with razors.
* Styling mastery: long-lasting blowouts, bridal/formal updos.
* Color: advanced foil placement, balayage/air-touch concepts (intro), color correction basics, grey blending vs coverage.
* Texture: Smoothing/keratin (intro, safety-first), perm waves (intro), deep conditioning sequences.
* Practicals: 20+ hair services (mix of cuts/colors/styling).

 Month 5: Makeup Artistry Deep-Dive
* Bridal packages and timelines, HD/photographic makeup, editorial concepts.
* Airbrush introduction (if available), hygiene and compressor basics.
* Collaboration: working with photographers/stylists; brief interpretation.
* Practicals: 10+ looks; 1 styled bridal shoot (team project).

 Month 6: Nails, Business & Capstone
* Nails: Gel polish systems, basic extensions (tip/overlay), refill/removal, sanitation, simple nail art.
* Business: Salon setup, licensing/insurance basics, budgeting, P&L basics, marketing funnels, memberships.
* Capstone: Plan and deliver a complete makeover project (skin + hair + makeup + nails) with costing, service flow, and client feedback.
* Practicals: 5+ nail services, 1 full capstone.

 Assessments
* Monthly theory quizzes + OSCE practicals.
* Final: Comprehensive viva + capstone evaluation + portfolio defense.
* Certification: Advanced Diploma in Cosmetology.

 5) Yoga & Fitness Instructor – 3 Months
* Delivery: 50% practical teaching labs, 30% theory, 20% supervised teaching
* Eligibility: Basic fitness level; medical self-declaration

 Module 1: Philosophy & Professional Ethics (12–14 hrs)
* Yoga history, schools (Hatha, Ashtanga, Iyengar basics), eight limbs.
* Scope of practice; non-diagnostic role; referral boundaries; inclusivity.

 Module 2: Anatomy & Physiology for Yoga (24–28 hrs)
* Musculoskeletal, respiratory, cardiovascular, nervous/endocrine basics.
* Biomechanics of common asanas; breath and HRV basics.
* Injury mechanisms; red flags; modifications and props.

 Module 3: Asana Labs (40–46 hrs)
* Warm-ups (Sukshma Vyayama), Surya Namaskar variants.
* Standing: Tadasana, Trikonasana, Virabhadrasana I/II/III, Ardha Chandrasana, etc.
* Seated: Dandasana, Paschimottanasana, Ardha Matsyendrasana, Baddha Konasana.
* Prone/Supine: Bhujangasana, Shalabhasana, Setu Bandha Sarvangasana.
* Balance/Core: Vrikshasana, Navasana, Kakasana progression.
* Inversions/Backbends: Sarvangasana, Halasana (intro), Ustrasana (safety-focused, optional).
* Sequencing by objective (strength/flexibility/relaxation).

 Module 4: Pranayama, Meditation & Relaxation (20–24 hrs)
* Pranayama: Dirgha, Ujjayi (intro), Nadi Shodhana, Bhramari, Kapalbhati/Bhastrika (contraindications).
* Meditation: breath awareness, mantra, body scan.
* Relaxation: Yoga Nidra scripts; savasana refinements.

 Module 5: Teaching Methodology (20–24 hrs)
* Class planning (45/60/90-min templates), cues, voice, mirroring.
* Hands-on/visual/verbal adjustments; prop use.
* Special populations: seniors, prenatal basics (intro), back pain management (non-therapeutic care), obesity considerations.
* Assessment tools: goal setting, progress logs.

 Module 6: Professional Practice (10–12 hrs)
* Client onboarding, consent, risk disclaimers.
* Business: pricing, packages, scheduling, community outreach.
* Basic first-aid/CPR awareness (recommend external certification).

 Assessments
* Teaching practicums (two evaluated classes).
* Theory MCQ + sequencing assignment.
* Final practical: lead a 60-min multi-level class.


 6) Diet & Nutrition Consultant – 4 Months
* Delivery: 40% theory, 40% applied planning, 20% casework/counseling
* Tools: Food models/portions, dietary analysis sheets, basic kitchen demo access (if possible)

 Module 1: Nutrition Science Foundations (24–28 hrs)
* Macronutrients: roles, sources, RDA/DRI concepts, quality (PDCAAS for proteins), fats (SFA/MUFA/PUFA, omega-3/6).
* Micronutrients: key vitamins/minerals; deficiency/toxicity; bioavailability.
* Digestion/absorption, glycemic response, energy balance, hydration/electrolytes.
* Reading food labels; additives; fortification.

 Module 2: Assessment & Tools (24–28 hrs)
* Anthropometry: height, weight, BMI, waist/hip; growth charts for children.
* Energy needs: BMR (Mifflin-St Jeor), PAL; TDEE.
* Dietary assessment: 24-hr recall, FFQ, food diaries; portion size estimation.
* Behavior assessment: readiness to change, barriers, cultural context.

 Module 3: Lifecycle & Lifestyle Nutrition (24–28 hrs)
* Infants/children: growth needs, picky eating strategies.
* Adolescents: sports, bone health, anemia prevention.
* Adults/older adults: sarcopenia, fiber, hydration.
* Women’s health: pregnancy/lactation basics, PCOS-friendly nutrition, iron/calcium strategies.
* Weight management: sustainable deficit/surplus planning.

 Module 4: Clinical & Therapeutic Nutrition (Intro) (30–36 hrs)
* Type 2 diabetes: carb quality, plate method, glycemic load; sample menus.
* Hypertension/dyslipidemia: DASH/Mediterranean patterns, sodium/fiber/plant sterols.
* Thyroid basics (hypothyroid-friendly patterns), gout awareness.
* GI-friendly diets (low-FODMAP intro), food intolerances (lactose/gluten awareness).
* Skin & hair nutrition (collagen cofactors; biotin, zinc, vitamin C).

 Module 5: Sports & Performance Nutrition (Intro) (14–18 hrs)
* Pre/intra/post fueling; hydration and electrolytes.
* Protein timing, creatine/caffeine basics; supplement safety overview.

 Module 6: Meal Planning & Culinary Skills (22–26 hrs)
* Meal frameworks (Indian regional templates + global options).
* Budget-friendly menus, batch-cooking, label-based grocery planning.
* Recipe modification: reduce sugar/salt/fat; increase fiber/protein.

 Module 7: Counseling, Ethics & Practice (18–22 hrs)
* Motivational interviewing basics; SMART goals; adherence tracking.
* Documentation, informed consent, scope boundaries and referrals.
* FSSAI basics (India), food safety (HACCP concepts at home level).

 Practicals & Casework
* Develop 10+ individualized diet charts (different ages/goals/conditions).
* Conduct 8–10 mock counseling sessions with feedback.
* Maintain client documentation and progress logs.

 Assessments
* Module quizzes; menu planning assignment; case-study viva.
* Final: Present 2 detailed case files with outcomes and follow-up plan.


 Cross-Cutting Standards (All Courses)
* Safety & Hygiene: Daily sanitation checks, PPE, MSDS awareness for chemicals, patch tests.
* Client Experience: Intake forms, consent, privacy, aftercare handouts.
* Professional Skills: Communication, sales with ethics, complaint handling, time management.
* Portfolio & Logs: Before/after photos (where appropriate), service counts, feedback records.
* Assessments: Theory + practicals + viva + professionalism rubric.
* Certification & Progression: Clear criteria; recommendations for higher study or specialization.
