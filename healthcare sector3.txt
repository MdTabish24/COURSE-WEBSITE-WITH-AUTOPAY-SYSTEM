Healthcare & Paramedical
1. General Duty Assistant (GDA)
Also known as "Nursing Aide" or "Patient Care Assistant."
•	Focus: Basic patient care, hygiene, and assisting nurses.
•	Duration: 6 Months (4 Months Classroom + 2 Months Internship)
Module 1: Introduction to Healthcare
•	Structure of a hospital and healthcare team.
•	Role and responsibilities of a GDA.
•	Medical Terminology basics.
•	Personal Hygiene and Professional Appearance.
Module 2: Anatomy & Physiology Basics
•	Basic understanding of human body systems (Skeletal, Muscular, Digestive, Respiratory, Circulatory).
•	Understanding body planes and cavities.
Module 3: Patient Care & Hygiene
•	Bathing the patient: Sponge bath, bed bath, assisted shower.
•	Grooming: Oral care, hair care, nail care, dressing/undressing.
•	Toileting: Use of bedpans, urinals, and diaper changing.
•	Prevention of Bedsores (Decubitus Ulcers): Back care and position changing.
Module 4: Vital Signs & Measurements
•	Measuring Temperature, Pulse, Respiration, and Blood Pressure (TPR & BP).
•	Measuring height and weight.
•	Charting and recording vitals.
Module 5: Patient Transfer & Mobility
•	Safe lifting techniques to prevent back injury.
•	Transferring patient: Bed to wheelchair, wheelchair to commode, stretcher transfer.
•	Use of walking aids (crutches, walkers, cane).
Module 6: Infection Control & Safety
•	Hand washing techniques (WHO guidelines).
•	Biomedical Waste Management (Color coding of bins).
•	Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) usage.
•	First Aid and Basic CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation).

2. Medical Lab Technician (MLT)
•	Focus: Diagnostic testing of blood, urine, and body fluids.
•	Duration: 1 Year (Diploma) or 2 Years (Advanced Diploma)
Module 1: Lab Management & Ethics
•	Laboratory organization and workflow.
•	Lab safety, glassware cleaning, and sterilization.
•	Biomedical Waste Management in the lab.
•	Medical ethics and patient confidentiality.
Module 2: Hematology (Blood Study)
•	Collection of blood (Phlebotomy): Venipuncture and capillary puncture.
•	Anticoagulants and their uses (EDTA, Citrate, Heparin).
•	Complete Blood Count (CBC): Hb, RBC, WBC, Platelet count.
•	ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate) and PCV.
•	Blood Grouping and Rh typing.
Module 3: Clinical Biochemistry
•	Blood Glucose estimation (Fasting, PP, Random).
•	Kidney Function Tests (Urea, Creatinine, Uric Acid).
•	Liver Function Tests (Bilirubin, SGOT, SGPT).
•	Lipid Profile (Cholesterol, Triglycerides).
•	Electrolytes (Sodium, Potassium).
Module 4: Clinical Pathology
•	Urine Analysis: Physical, Chemical (Strip test), and Microscopic examination.
•	Stool Analysis: Colour, consistency, and parasite detection.
•	Semen analysis (Basic).
Module 5: Microbiology & Serology
•	Staining techniques: Gram stain, AFB (ZN) stain.
•	Sterilization methods (Autoclave, Hot air oven).
•	Serology tests: Typhoid (Widal), Dengue, Malaria, HIV/HBsAg (Rapid kits).

3. Radiology & X-Ray Technician
•	Focus: Diagnostic imaging (X-Ray) and radiation safety.
•	Duration: 1 to 2 Years
Module 1: Human Anatomy & Physiology
•	Detailed study of the Skeletal System (Bones and Joints).
•	Surface anatomy and landmarks for positioning.
Module 2: Radiation Physics & Safety
•	Properties of X-Rays.
•	Production of X-Rays (X-Ray tube construction).
•	Radiation Protection: ALARA principle, Lead aprons, TLD badges, shielding.
•	Darkroom procedures: Processing, developing, and fixing films (Manual vs. Digital).
Module 3: Radiographic Positioning (Practical Core)
•	Upper Limb: Hand, wrist, forearm, elbow, humerus, shoulder.
•	Lower Limb: Foot, ankle, leg, knee, femur, hip.
•	Spine: Cervical, Thoracic, Lumbar, Sacrum/Coccyx.
•	Chest & Abdomen: Chest PA/AP, Abdomen erect/supine.
•	Skull: Basic views.
Module 4: Modern Imaging Technologies
•	Introduction to CR (Computed Radiography) and DR (Digital Radiography).
•	Basics of CT Scan, MRI, and Ultrasound (Introduction only).
•	Contrast Media: Types, uses, and adverse reactions.

4. Operation Theatre Technician (OTT)
•	Focus: Managing the OT, sterilization, and assisting surgeons/anesthetists.
•	Duration: 1 Year
Module 1: OT Organization
•	Zoning in OT (Protective, Clean, Aseptic, Disposal zones).
•	OT Environment: Temperature, humidity, lighting, ventilation (HEPA filters).
•	Role of the Scrub Nurse and Circulating Nurse.
Module 2: Infection Control & Sterilization (CSSD)
•	Methods of Sterilization: Autoclaving (Steam), ETO (Gas), Chemical sterilization.
•	Disinfection of OT tables, lights, and floor (Fumigation procedures).
•	Packing and wrapping of surgical drums/trays.
Module 3: Anesthesia Basics
•	Types of Anesthesia: General, Regional (Spinal/Epidural), Local.
•	Anesthesia Machine: Parts, cylinders (Color codes: O2-White, N2O-Blue), circuits.
•	Intubation equipment: Laryngoscope, Endotracheal tubes.
•	Patient monitoring during surgery (Pulse oximetry, ECG, Capnography).
Module 4: Surgical Instruments & Procedures
•	Identification of instruments: Forceps, Scissors, Retractors, Scalpels, Needle holders.
•	Trolley setup for minor and major surgeries.
•	Suturing materials: Absorbable vs. Non-absorbable, needle types.
Module 5: Patient Care in OT
•	Pre-operative preparation (Checklist).
•	Intra-operative positioning (Supine, Prone, Lithotomy, Lateral).
•	Post-operative transfer to recovery room.

5. Pharmacy Assistant
•	Focus: Dispensing medicines, inventory management, and patient counseling.
•	Duration: 6 Months to 1 Year
Module 1: Introduction to Pharmacy
•	History of Pharmacy.
•	Indian Pharmacopoeia (IP), BP, USP concepts.
•	Pharmacy Act and Drugs & Cosmetics Act (Basics).
Module 2: Pharmaceutics (Dosage Forms)
•	Solid dosage forms: Tablets, Capsules, Powders.
•	Liquid dosage forms: Syrups, Suspensions, Emulsions.
•	Semisolid: Ointments, Creams, Gels.
•	Sterile formulations: Injections, Eye drops.
Module 3: Pharmacology Basics
•	Routes of drug administration (Oral, IV, IM, Subcutaneous).
•	Common Drug Classes: Antibiotics, Analgesics (Painkillers), Antipyretics, Antacids, Vitamins.
•	Storage conditions (Cold chain management for insulin/vaccines).
Module 4: Community Pharmacy & Dispensing
•	Reading and understanding prescriptions.
•	Labeling of dispensed medicines.
•	Patient counseling: How to take medicine, side effects.
•	Calculation of doses (Pediatric dose calculations).
Module 5: Inventory Management
•	Stock maintenance, FIFO (First In, First Out) & FEFO (First Expiry, First Out).
•	Handling expired drugs.
•	Computer usage for billing and inventory.

6. Nursing Assistant
•	Focus: Advanced patient care, assisting in clinical procedures (More medical than GDA).
•	Duration: 1 Year
Module 1: Fundamentals of Nursing
•	Nursing process (Assessment, Diagnosis, Planning, Implementation, Evaluation).
•	Documentation and reporting.
•	Admission and Discharge procedures.
Module 2: Anatomy & Physiology
•	Detailed study of body systems.
•	Fluid and Electrolyte balance.
Module 3: Basic Nursing Procedures
•	Vital Signs: Advanced monitoring.
•	Medication Administration: Oral, Topical, Rectal (Assisting in Injections).
•	Oxygen Therapy: Nasal cannula, mask, cylinder handling.
•	Nebulization: Procedure and care.
•	Hot and Cold Applications: Ice packs, hot water bags.
Module 4: Wound Care & Dressing
•	Types of wounds.
•	Aseptic dressing techniques.
•	Bandaging techniques (Spiral, Figure of 8, Capeline).
Module 5: Special Care
•	Care of unconscious patients.
•	Care of terminally ill patients.
•	Catheter care (Assisting in catheterization).
•	Ryle’s Tube (Nasogastric) feeding assistance.

7. Dental Assistant
•	Focus: Assisting dentists, dental materials, and clinic management.
•	Duration: 6 Months to 1 Year
Module 1: Dental Anatomy
•	Structure of a tooth (Enamel, Dentin, Pulp).
•	Types of teeth (Incisors, Canines, Premolars, Molars).
•	Tooth numbering systems (FDI/Universal).
Module 2: Dental Equipment & Chair-side Assistance
•	Dental Chair operation.
•	Understanding the dental unit (Suction, Air-rotor, Micromotor).
•	Instrument transfer techniques (Four-handed dentistry).
•	Isolation techniques (Cotton rolls, Rubber dam).
Module 3: Dental Materials
•	Dental Cements (Mixing techniques).
•	Impression materials (Alginate, Silicone).
•	Filling materials (Composite, Amalgam).
Module 4: Dental Radiology
•	Intra-oral X-ray techniques (IOPA).
•	Radiation safety in the dental clinic.
•	Developing dental X-ray films.
Module 5: Sterilization & Infection Control
•	Sterilization of dental handpieces and instruments.
•	Disposal of needles and blades.

8. Emergency Medical Technician (EMT)
•	Focus: Pre-hospital emergency care, ambulance operations, life support.
•	Duration: 6 Months to 1 Year
Module 1: Introduction to EMS (Emergency Medical Services)
•	Roles and responsibilities of an EMT.
•	Medico-legal aspects (Consent, Do Not Resuscitate orders).
•	Human Anatomy relevant to trauma.
Module 2: Airway & Respiratory Management
•	Airway obstruction management (Heimlich maneuver).
•	Use of Oropharyngeal (OPA) and Nasopharyngeal (NPA) airways.
•	Suctioning techniques.
•	Oxygen administration devices.
Module 3: Basic Life Support (BLS)
•	CPR (Adult, Child, Infant) - AHA Guidelines.
•	Use of AED (Automated External Defibrillator).
•	Recovery position.
Module 4: Trauma Management
•	Bleeding control (Tourniquets, Pressure bandages).
•	Shock management.
•	Musculoskeletal injuries: Splinting fractures, Cervical collar application.
•	Head and Spinal injuries (Log roll technique).
•	Burns management.
Module 5: Medical Emergencies
•	Cardiac emergencies (Heart attack/Angina).
•	Respiratory emergencies (Asthma/COPD).
•	Diabetic emergencies (Hypoglycemia/Hyperglycemia).
•	Stroke (CVA) recognition.
•	Poisoning and Snake bites.
Module 6: Ambulance Operations
•	Safe ambulance driving.
•	Loading and unloading patients (Stretcher operations).
•	Communication with the hospital/dispatch.
